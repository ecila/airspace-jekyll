ECOLISTENING

Interdisciplinary Soundscape Research at the University of Sussex

Learning to Listen to Soundscape

We are a small interdisciplinary team working on applied and experimental soundscape research.

Soundscape refers to all the sounds emanating from a given environment -- sounds produced and shaped by humans and non-human species, weather and the landscape itself, in both wild and built environments. Our soundscapes are therefore both the literal and conceptual site of interaction of humans and other animals; learning to listen to soundscape provides a revolutionary way of understanding the activities and interactions of all actors in our environments.

Based across schools at the University of Sussex, we work at the intersections of conservation biology, computer science & engineering, social science and music. We are working on the development, evaluation and application of new soundscape research methods. This includes new hardware, software and methodologies for learning to listen in new ways.
